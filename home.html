Welcome to NeoFOCUS-UK

Advancing Neonatal Functional Haemodynamic and Point of Care Ultrasound in the UK

About NeoFOCUS-UK

At NeoFOCUS-UK (Neonatal Functional Haemodynamics and Point of Care Ultrasound), we are a dedicated group of UK-based neonatologists, specializing in the application of functional echocardiography and point of care ultrasound (POCUS) in neonatal care. Our mission is to enhance diagnostic precision and support pathophysiology-based neonatal management in term and preterm infants. With growing evidence supporting the benefits of POCUS, we aim to overcome barriers to training and governance in the UK neonatology community.


Why NeoFOCUS-UK?

International surveys consistently reveal the desire among neonatologists to incorporate POCUS into their clinical practice. In the UK, our recent survey indicated a significant interest in learning these valuable skills. While POCUS and echocardiography training programs are well-established for adult and paediatric critical care, there are no accepted standards for training, accreditation, or application of these techniques in neonatal settings in the UK.


Our Expertise

NeoFOCUS-UK comprises members with internationally recognized expertise in neonatal lung ultrasound, neonatal echocardiography, functional haemodynamic assessment, and ultrasound confirmation of central line position. Many of our members contribute to international training courses, teaching, and guideline development through their involvement with organizations such as the Neonatal Hemodynamic Research Centre (NHRC), European Society of Pediatric and Neonatal Intensive Care, and the CACTUS (Children’s Acute US) POCUS courses within the UK.

Advancing NeoFOCUS-UK: A Proposal for Specialist Interest Group within BAPM

Building on our success, we have proposed establishing NeoFOCUS-UK as a Specialist Interest Group within British Association of Perinatal Medicine:

Building on our accomplishments, we propose the establishment of NeoFOCUS-UK as a Specialist Interest Group (SIG) within the British Association of Perinatal Medicine (BAPM). British Association of Perinatal Medicine | Home (bapm.org) This initiative aligns with our mission to develop national training and clinical practice frameworks, involve the broader neonatology community, and promote our endeavours through BAPM's established channels.

As a SIG, we propose to undertake various activities, including:

  1. 1. Organizational Structures and Frameworks: Developing organizational structures and frameworks for practice.
  2. 2. Guidelines: Crafting condition-specific guidelines, including neonatal lung ultrasound, integrated haemodynamic assessment, and POCUS for central line assessment.
  3. 3. Training: Creating a core curriculum, establishing training requirements, developing a robust process for credentialing, and delivering hands-on training courses.
  4. 4. Continuing Professional Development: Offering regular CPD and peer-review sessions for clinicians using POCUS and integrated haemodynamic skills.
  5. 5. Research and Quality Improvement: Engaging in research and quality improvement initiatives related to POCUS and integrated haemodynamics in neonatology.
  6. 6. We also aim to include patient and family representatives, neonatal trainee representatives, and relevant specialist expertise.

The membership of the SIG will encompass:

  1. - NeoFOCUS-UK members who are also BAPM members.
  2. - BAPM members with relevant expertise or a specialist interest.
  3. - Patient and family representatives.
  4. - Neonatal trainee representatives.
  5. -Additionally, we will collaborate with experts in radiology, cardiology, adult and paediatric critical care, including PECSIG and CACTUS leadership, to develop the training curriculum and practice guidelines.

By becoming a SIG within BAPM, we anticipate:

  1. - Engagement and Inclusion: Involving the broader BAPM community in NeoFOCUS-UK's activities.
  2. - Promotion: Utilizing BAPM channels to promote our activities and outputs, particularly in training and frameworks.
  3. - Coordination: Receiving assistance from BAPM to coordinate NeoFOCUS-UK activities.
  4. - Support at Meetings: Gaining support for NeoFOCUS-UK-led sessions at BAPM and RCPCH annual meetings.
  5. - Curriculum Inclusion: Working towards the inclusion of accredited POCUS and integrated haemodynamic training in the neonatal specialist curriculum.

Funding

NeoFOCUS-UK operates with a modest non-profit fund managed within Glasgow Children's Hospital Charity, providing a structured financial platform for future training courses and related activities.
